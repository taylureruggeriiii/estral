Step 3 of 5 · Your health

is there anything going on hormonally or physically that shapes your daily experience? this changes how i read your patterns and what i suggest.

Your body,
fully.
◑
PCOS
Polycystic ovary syndrome — irregular cycles, insulin resistance, androgen excess
with PCOS, your cycle doesn't follow a predictable rhythm — and that's not a failure, it's a different kind of data. i won't predict your phases. i'll track your symptoms and energy and build your picture from what's actually happening. everything i suggest around food will prioritize blood sugar stability — that's the foundation for PCOS management.
◑
Endometriosis
Tissue growing outside the uterus — pain, inflammation, hormonal disruption
endo is an inflammatory condition — which means inflammation is the lens i'll use for everything i suggest. food, movement, rest. anti-inflammatory will be my default. i'll also watch your pain and symptom logs closely and flag patterns you might not have connected yet.
◑
Breastfeeding / postpartum
Currently nursing or recently postpartum — hormones, cycle, and body are in transition
you're feeding two people right now. that changes everything — caloric needs, hydration, what your body is prioritizing. i won't suggest restriction or anything that undermines supply. your nourishment is her nourishment. cycle tracking is paused until you tell me your period has returned — until then we use the moon as your primary rhythm anchor.
◑
On hormonal birth control
The pill, patch, ring, hormonal IUD, implant, or injection
hormonal contraception suppresses your natural cycle — the four phases i normally track don't apply the same way. i'll use the moon as your primary rhythm instead, and your symptom and energy logs as the real data. if you have withdrawal bleeds, i'll note them but won't treat them as true menstrual cycles.
◑
Thyroid condition
Hypothyroid, hyperthyroid, Hashimoto's, or Graves' disease
thyroid function affects your energy, metabolism, mood, and cycle — sometimes dramatically. i'll pay close attention to your energy patterns and flag anything that looks like a thyroid signal versus a cycle signal. i'll also surface iodine and selenium-rich foods which support thyroid function, and flag goitrogenic foods that can interfere with absorption.
◑
IBS / gut issues
Irritable bowel syndrome, Crohn's, colitis, or chronic digestive issues
the gut-hormone connection is real and significant — your cycle directly affects your gut motility, which is why symptoms often shift with your phase. i'll track your digestive logs carefully and connect them to where you are in your cycle. food suggestions will avoid common IBS triggers and prioritize gut-supportive options.

ESTRAL is not a medical app and nothing here is medical advice. This information only changes how i talk to you and what i suggest — not diagnose or treat anything.

Estrogen rising.

You're in the follicular phase — the rebuilding window that follows your period. Your body has done a full reset, and now it's building back.

Estrogen is climbing steadily. As it rises, it stimulates serotonin and dopamine — so you naturally feel more motivated, more social, more optimistic. This is biological, not personality.

FSH (follicle-stimulating hormone) is also active right now, triggering your ovaries to mature eggs. Your brain literally grows new neural connections faster during this phase than any other.

What this means for you today: sharper thinking, better verbal recall, more creative bandwidth, more willingness to take risks. This is a high-output window. Your mind wants to build and start things.

The trap: overcommitting. You feel good so you say yes to everything. Don't. Protect the energy for what actually matters.
